SymmDiff Symmetry-Guided Diffusion for Fracture
Localization in Pelvic Radiographs

A symmetry-aware diffusion framework that embeds contralateral consistency directly into the generative correction process — mirroring the clinical practice of bilateral comparison to localize pelvic fractures through input–reconstruction discrepancies.

01 Abstract

Unsupervised anomaly detection (UAD) offers a promising direction for fracture analysis where abnormal annotations are scarce, yet pelvic X-ray fracture detection remains exceptionally challenging due to overlapping anatomy, projection artifacts, and the inherently asymmetric presentation of fracture patterns.

We introduce SymmDiff, a symmetry-aware diffusion framework that embeds contralateral anatomical consistency directly into the generative correction process, mirroring the clinical practice of bilateral comparison. By pairing anatomically corresponding left–right regions and enforcing symmetry-consistent reconstruction during diffusion, SymmDiff generates a normal-corrected radiograph that selectively suppresses fracture-induced asymmetries while preserving global anatomical structure, enabling precise fracture localization through residual discrepancies between the input image and its symmetry-corrected counterpart.

Extensive evaluations on pelvic radiographs spanning subtle to severe fractures show that SymmDiff consistently outperforms existing anomaly-detection baselines, highlighting the effectiveness of symmetry-guided generative modeling for reliable fracture localization in clinical imaging.

02 Key Contributions

i.

Symmetry-conditioned latent diffusion

A latent diffusion model that establishes a patient-specific representation of normal pelvic anatomy through contralateral correspondence, providing a structural prior for fracture localization.

ii.

Reverse-process symmetry coupling

A symmetry-conditioned reverse diffusion formulation in which contralateral information guides the reverse transition at every step, stabilizing the anatomical trajectory during iterative denoising.

iii.

ROI-guided reverse diffusion

A scheme with contralateral latent-swap initialization that softly restricts updates to regions of elevated bilateral discrepancy, reducing projection-induced over-correction while preserving global anatomy.

03 Method

SymmDiff couples a flipped contralateral reference into the denoising trajectory of a latent diffusion model. Training enforces symmetry-consistent reconstruction under random patch masking; inference performs ROI-targeted correction initialized by a latent swap, followed by deterministic DDIM sampling. A multi-scale discrepancy fusion turns the input–reconstruction gap into a localized anomaly map.

01

Latent space & symmetry mapping

Input x and its flipped reference xsym=𝒯(x) are encoded by a pelvic-finetuned VAE to latents z, zsym. Conditioning on the contralateral latent reduces uncertainty, i.e. H(Z∣R) < H(Z), giving the generator a patient-specific structural anchor.

02

Symmetry-constrained training

A conditional U-Net εθ predicts noise on patch-masked latents while attending to zsym via channel concatenation, forcing the network to inpaint masked regions using bilateral context rather than memorizing local textures.

03

ROI-targeted reconstruction

At test time, a soft ROI mask derived from left–right discrepancy gates the reverse trajectory. A latent swap inside the ROI biases the trajectory toward the symmetry-consistent manifold, and DDIM denoising updates only inside the ROI, preserving global anatomy.

04

Multi-scale anomaly fusion

Four complementary residuals — pixel, gradient, perceptual, and latent — are normalized and fused into a single anomaly map, capturing thin cortical breaks as well as broader deformities.

04 Results

Quantitative comparison

SymmDiff consistently outperforms recent diffusion-based UAD baselines on the pelvic X-ray benchmark at both image and pixel levels. Relative to the second-best method (THOR), image-level AUROC / AUPRC / F1max improve by +4.9 / +2.5 / +2.8, and pixel-level by +3.9 / +7.7 / +5.5.

Qualitative localization

Across minor-to-large fractures, SymmDiff produces anatomically plausible reconstructions and tightly localized anomaly maps. For an implant case, activation concentrates around the implant with minimal spillover; for a healthy case, activations remain low.

Ablations

Every component contributes. Removing symmetry conditioning causes the largest drop, confirming the side-to-side prior as the strongest stabilizer; ROI-constrained denoising prevents global drift, and multi-scale fusion is necessary for thin fractures that are weak in raw intensity residuals.

05 Takeaways

01

Contralateral consistency is a strong inductive bias for pelvic UAD — it stabilizes “normality” where global density alone fails.

02

Coupling symmetry into the reverse trajectory — not post-hoc — prevents anatomical drift during iterative denoising.

03

ROI-gated, identity-outside-mask updates make the pipeline robust to imperfect contralateral references.

04

Multi-scale residual fusion is essential for thin cortical breaks that vanish in any single residual view.
